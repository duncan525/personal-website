Brackeys is a well-known and beloved YouTube channel about game development. They have tutorials for a wide range of technical concepts across a variety of platforms (particularly Unity), but they have design-oriented tutorials as well.

I became familiar with Brackeys long before I learned how to code. Their content definitely has entertainment value -- particularly the less-technical design videos -- so it's worth checking out even if you're not interested in game development.

You can find more information at brackeys.com.

GMTK, or Game Maker's Toolkit, is a YouTube channel focused on "game design, level design, and game production," according to their channel description. I didn't use GMTK as a resource during the project, but they hosted the game jam my friend and I made the game for, which is why I include them.

I've watched GMTK for several years as pure entertainment. I'm enough of a fan to have kept up with previous jams, so it was really cool to participate in one! I enjoy playing games, watching other people play games, and listening to other people talk about games. Making one is a natural next step for me, and one I've wanted to take for a long time.

LearnCode.academy is a YouTube channel focused on web development tutorials. They cover a variety of concepts including (but not limited to) APIs, languages, and libraries. Their last video was posted in April of 2022, so I don't know if they're still active.

W3Schools hosts web development resources, tutorials, and exercises for learners. They also offer paid courses and certifications. They were not the only text-based resource I used as I made the project, but they were the only one I visited consistently, which is why I list them here.

  • "How to make a Video Game" is a comprehensive introduction to the Godot game engine. You'll learn the ins and outs of important systems and interactions in the engine by creating a small game!
  • "How to program in Godot" is an introduction to Godot's built-in scripting language, GDScript. It's geared towards non-coders and beginners. If you're familiar with Python and object-oriented programming, it'll be a quick skim.
    • GMTK hosts an annual game jam -- the largest in the world, in fact. Participants must make a game within the 5-day time window according to a theme (2025's theme was "loop"). Entries are ranked by the public, and the highest-ranking ones are judged by GMTK.
    • "Web Development Tutorials for Beginners" is a playlist of short videos for different aspects of web development. I used it to get started with HTML, CSS, and Javascript.
    • For my purposes, W3Schools' CSS Reference is an easier-to-read version of Mozilla's CSS documentation. They also have references for HTML and Javascript (among others), which I used less often.
